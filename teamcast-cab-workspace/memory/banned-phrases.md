# Banned Phrases

> Phrases that must never appear in outbound emails. Enforced by the QA Agent.

---

## Permanently Banned

### Generic Flattery
- "Impressed by your leadership"
- "Impressed by your work at [Company]"
- "I've been following your career"
- "I admire what you've built"
- "You're a thought leader in..."

### Hype Language
- "Game-changer"
- "Revolutionary"
- "Disruptive"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Best-in-class"
- "World-class"
- "Next-generation"

### Pressure Tactics
- "Don't miss this opportunity"
- "Limited spots remaining"
- "Act now"
- "This offer expires"
- "You won't want to miss this"
- "Before it's too late"

### False Intimacy
- "As a fellow [anything]"
- "I know exactly what you're going through"
- "We're a lot alike"

### Guilt Framing
- "I know you're busy, but..."
- "I hate to bother you..."
- "Sorry to take up your time..."
- "I promise this will be quick..."

### Weak Filler
- "I am sure you may have questions"
- "I'd love to pick your brain"
- "Just circling back"
- "Just checking in"
- "Hope this finds you well"
- "Hope you're having a great week"
- "I wanted to reach out because..."

### Explicit Sales Language
- "Buy"
- "Purchase"
- "Demo"
- "Free trial"
- "Special offer"
- "Discount"
- "ROI"
- "Revenue growth"

## Conditionally Banned (Context-Dependent)
- "Board seat invite" as subject line — allowed but use sparingly, can sound governance-like
- "Tomorrow or Tuesday" — banned as static text, must be converted to actual dates
